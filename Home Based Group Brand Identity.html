Brand System & Guidelines
01Brand Strategy

A house
of provenance.

Home Based Group is not an agricultural company that trades. It is a trading house built on the discipline of agriculture — one that treats provenance, certification, and relationship as its true product.

The strategy rests on a single organising idea: provenance made portable. Every element of the identity — from the meridian mark to the estate palette — exists to make the origin of a product legible, credible, and desirable anywhere in the world. This is what allows a holding company spanning grain, wine, and olive oil to feel singular rather than scattered.

We position the brand at the intersection of two visual worlds that rarely meet: the Mediterranean estate — warm, editorial, tactile — and the global trading corporation — precise, navigational, institutional. The tension between these is the brand's signature, and it is resolved through restraint.

i.

The Insight

Buyers of premium commodities no longer purchase on price alone. They purchase on trust in origin. The brand that documents provenance most beautifully wins the contract.

ii.

The Idea

Provenance made portable. One rigorous visual grammar carries the weight of the estate into the boardroom, the port, and the shelf without dilution.

iii.

The Ambition

To become the reference standard for African-origin premium agriculture in global markets — a name that reassures investors and elevates producers alike.

02Positioning
The Statement

For international buyers, investors and partners who demand certainty of origin, Home Based Group is the premium multinational agribusiness that connects Africa's agricultural excellence to global markets — because it treats integrity, quality assurance and sustainability not as claims, but as documented, repeatable practice.

Competitive Frame

We benchmark our credibility against the great trading and premium-producer houses — Cargill, Louis Dreyfus, Olam, ADM, ECOM, Deoleo, Penfolds, Swire — while deliberately owning territory none of them occupy: the articulate, design-led expression of African provenance at global standard.

03Brand Story · Purpose · Mission · Vision

The line
that connects.

Every estate has a horizon. Every trade route has a meridian. Home Based Group exists on the line where the two meet — where what is grown with care in one place is made trustworthy everywhere.

The name itself carries the philosophy. Home is provenance — the soil, the grove, the vineyard, the people who tend them. Group is reach — the network, the vessels, the standards that carry that home to the world. The brand is the discipline of keeping the two honest to one another: nothing leaves the home that would not be welcomed as excellent abroad; nothing is promised abroad that the home cannot deliver.

This is a house built for the long horizon. Agriculture rewards patience; trade rewards reliability; luxury rewards consistency. The identity is engineered to age like the products it represents — to look as considered in three decades as it does today.

Purpose

To make the excellence of African agriculture undeniable in the markets that matter most.

Mission

To source, refine, certify and deliver premium agricultural products — with provenance documented and partnership honoured — from origin to global shelf.

Vision

A world that trusts African provenance as instinctively as it trusts any great estate — and a group known as the reason why.

04Values & Personality

Six commitments.
One character.

Values are not adjectives on a wall. Each is written as a promise the group can be held to — and each maps to a behaviour the brand must demonstrate in every touchpoint.

  • Integrity

    We document what we claim. Provenance, grading and certification are shown, not asserted — because trust that can be verified is the only trust worth trading on.

  • Provenance

    Origin is our value. We protect the story of the soil and the hands behind every product, and we make it legible across languages and borders.

  • Stewardship

    We farm and trade for the next harvest, not only this one. Sustainability is measured, reported, and built into the economics — not bolted on for appearance.

  • Craft

    From a barrel of wine to a bill of lading, we treat the detail as the product. Precision is a form of respect for the partner on the other side.

  • Partnership

    We grow by making others succeed — producers, distributors, governments, investors. The relationship outlives the transaction.

  • Endurance

    We build for decades. Stability is our promise to everyone who plants, ships, invests or partners on the strength of our name.

Brand Personality — if HBG were a person

A seasoned diplomat who happens to be a master vintner: unhurried, exact, warm without being familiar, and completely at ease in both a Mediterranean cellar and a ministerial boardroom. Cultured, never flashy. Confident enough to be quiet.

05Tone of Voice & Messaging

We write
like we trade.

Precise, warm, and unexaggerated. The voice earns trust by under-claiming and over-delivering — it never sells what the products already prove.

Four voice principles

  • Speak in evidence.

    Numbers, certifications and origins over adjectives. "Cold-pressed within four hours of harvest" beats "the finest quality."

  • Be the calm in the room.

    Short, composed sentences. We never sound anxious to impress.

  • Honour both sides.

    Producer and buyer are named with equal respect. Partnership language, not conquest language.

  • Let the noun carry it.

    Estate, harvest, meridian, provenance. Concrete words do the work that hype cannot.

07Logo Concept I
Primary — The Official Mark

The HBG
Signature.

The definitive Home Base Group mark: an interlocking H·B·G monogram rising into a single leaf, set above the full name. Heritage, growth, and enterprise resolved into one confident lockup.

Rationale

The three initials are woven into a continuous line — no letter stands alone, expressing a single house of many disciplines. The leaf growing from the crossbar roots the group in agriculture, provenance, and living things, while the elegant capitals beneath give it the authority of an international trading corporation.

The mark is delivered in three master colourways — Olive Green, Antique Gold, and Warm White — so it holds its dignity on any surface. Each colourway is chosen by context: the green mark on light grounds, the white mark on dark or photographic grounds, and the gold mark for premium, ceremonial, and foil applications.

08Logo Concept II
Concept Exploration — Symbolic Mark

The
Meridian.

A single olive form drawn as a globe. One vertical meridian bisects it; the outer ring is the world, the inner leaf is the estate. Navigation and cultivation resolve into one geometry.

Rationale

The mark answers the brief's central tension. Read one way it is a globe with a meridian line — international trade, navigation, the reach of a group. Read another it is a single olive or grape in cross-section with its stem — agriculture, wine, olive oil, growth. The two readings share the same lines, so the symbol literally embodies "provenance made portable."

It uses no leaf clipart, no generic globe, no gradient. It is pure geometry — a compass rose compressed into a seed. It works at 16px as a favicon and embosses cleanly into glass, foil and leather. The central gold node is the meeting point: home and world, origin and market.

09Logo Concept III
Ceremonial — Heritage Seal

The Estate
Crest.

A modern seal, not a coat of arms. A thin ring holds a horizon line above furrowed field geometry — the view from the estate — with the meridian rising through it. For wax seals, foil, capsules and certificates.

Rationale

Great agricultural and wine houses earn authority through the language of the seal — but the brief rightly forbids clichéd heraldry. This crest is reduced to essentials: a horizon (the estate overlooking vineyards), field lines in perspective (cultivation, geometry, not clipart rows), and the meridian tying it to the primary mark.

It is the identity's ceremonial register — reserved for certification seals, wine capsules, embossed folios, and government documents. It signals legacy and authenticity without a single leaf or wheat sheaf. The ring carries the legal name and founding year in the trusted convention of the great houses.

11Construction · Clearspace · Misuse
Clearspace & minimum size

Minimum clearspace on all sides equals the cap-height (X) of the HBG monogram element. Minimum sizes: primary Signature 30mm / 120px wide · Meridian icon 12mm / 40px · Estate Crest 22mm / 72px.

Construction grid

The Meridian is built on a circle of radius 3.5 grid-units; the olive form spans the full diameter; the meridian and node sit on the central axis. Stroke weight is constant across all elements.

Correct usage
  • Maintain the logo's original proportions — never redraw it
  • Use the three approved colourways: Olive, Gold and White
  • Preserve full clearspace on all applications
  • Choose the colourway by context — Olive on light, White on dark, Gold for premium
  • Use the Estate Crest for seals and the Meridian for icon-only moments
Never do this
  • Never add gradients, shadows or bevels to the mark
  • Never stretch, rotate or recolour outside the palette
  • Never fill the olive form with solid colour or imagery
  • Never place the mark on a busy photo without a scrim
  • Never recreate the wordmark in a substitute typeface
  • Never combine two marks in a single lockup

17Pattern · Texture · Graphic Elements

The graticule.

The brand's connective tissue is the graticule — the fine grid of latitude and longitude derived from the meridian mark. It appears as embossed backgrounds, section dividers, endpapers and packaging texture. Two supporting devices: field-furrow lines and the horizon rule.

GRATICULE
FIELD FURROWS
HORIZON RULE
Texture

Uncoated linen-weave stock, blind emboss, deckled edges. Physical tactility is part of the luxury language.

Application

Graticule as watermark on reports; furrows as section breaks; horizon rule under every headline.

Restraint

Patterns sit at 8–20% opacity. They frame content; they never crowd it. Whitespace is a brand asset.

18Photography Direction

Golden hour,
always.

Cinematic, warm, and authentic — the register of a luxury annual report, not a stock library. Natural light, shallow depth of field, honest hands and materials. Every image should feel like a still from a documentary about excellence.

The four families
  • Origin

    Olive groves & vineyards at golden hour, harvest hands, soil, the estate landscape.

  • Craft

    Wine production, oil pouring, inspection close-ups, packaging detail — the making.

  • Movement

    Modern ports, containers, logistics, warehouses — trade rendered cinematically.

  • People

    Executive partnership, considered portraits — dignified, never staged smiles.

Grade & treatment

Warm highlights, deep olive-brown shadows, gentle contrast. Colour graded toward the estate palette — desaturated greens, honeyed light, never neon. Film grain welcome; over-sharpening forbidden.

Never
  • Generic smiling farmers / posed handshakes
  • Low-quality or clearly-stock imagery
  • Cartoon agriculture or clip-art overlays
  • Over-saturated greens & busy compositions
